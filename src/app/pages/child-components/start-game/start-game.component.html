Color Theory

RGB color theory is a fundamental concept in digital color representation. It revolves around the additive mixture of red, green, and blue light at varying intensities to create a wide range of colors. By combining different levels of these primary colors, RGB color theory forms the basis for how electronic displays, such as monitors and screens, render colors accurately and produce the vibrant visuals we perceive.
